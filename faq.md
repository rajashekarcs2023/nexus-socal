General
What is this hackathon?
A one-day intercollegiate AI hackathon hosted by UCLA, USC, Caltech, and Nexus at UCLA's Ackerman Grand Ballroom on April 19, 2026. 100 student builders competing to build AI-powered projects for social impact, powered by Anthropic.
Who can apply?
Students from UCLA, USC, and Caltech are welcome to apply. All majors and experience levels are encouraged — this is intentionally inclusive and beginner-friendly.
Is it free? Yes — completely free to attend. Lunch and snacks are provided.
When and where? April 19, 2026 | 8:30AM–6:30PM | Grand Ackerman Ballroom, UCLA
Timeline
When is the application deadline? Friday, April 3rd (hard deadline Monday, April 6th).
When will I hear back? Decisions will be released Wednesday, April 8th. Accepted hackers will be invited to a Slack and sent their Claude Pro link.
What's the day-of schedule?
● 8:30AM — Check-in opens
● 9:15AM — Opening ceremony
● 9:40AM — Team matching
● 10:00AM — Hacking begins
● 12:00PM — Lunch
● 3:00PM — Snacks
● 4:00PM — Submissions due (hard stop 4:15PM)
● 4:30PM — Judging
● 6:00PM — Winners announced & closing ceremony
● 6:20PM — Networking & Wrap-up
Teams
What's the team size? Teams of 2–4 people.
What if I don't have a team? No problem — there will be a dedicated team matching session at 9:40AM. Organizers will actively help connect solo hackers with each other, so you won't be left without a team.
Can I apply solo? Yes — if you apply solo, we'll make sure you're matched with a team during the team matching session on the day.
Building
What tools will I have access to? All accepted hackers will receive free Claude Pro and API credits before the hackathon. You'll also have access to Anthropic Academy resources, in-person mentors, and Anthropic Campus Ambassadors throughout the day.
Do I have to use Claude? Yes — this is a Claude hackathon. Projects should meaningfully use Claude's capabilities.
Can I use other tools and APIs alongside Claude? Yes. The hackathon is powered by Anthropic, Supabase, and Fetch.ai — you're encouraged to use any tools that help you build the best project.
Can I use hardware or physical components? No — this is a software-only hackathon. All projects should be built entirely in software.
Do I need to start from scratch on the day? Yes — projects must be built during hack time on April 19th.
What tracks can I build in?
There are three tracks to use as jumping-off points:
● 🎨 Creative Flourishing
● 📚 Economic Empowerment & Education
● 🩺 Health & Wellbeing
Tracks are reference points, not hard constraints. Build what matters to you.
Submission
How do I submit my project? All projects are submitted through Devpost before the 4:00PM deadline. Make sure your submission is in before time is called — late submissions will not be accepted.
What happens after submission? After submitting on Devpost, your team will demo your project live to assigned judges during the judging period (4:30–5:30PM). Judges will visit your table and watch a 5-minute demo.
Do I need a slide deck or video? No slide deck or pre-recorded video is required. You'll demo your working project live to judges.
Judging & Prizes
How are projects judged?
Each project is assigned 2 judges who visit your table, watch a 5-minute live demo, and score independently using a rubric. Scores are averaged for the fi nal ranking. Top 3 overall prizes are determined automatically by score. Category prizes are decided by judge discussion after all demos wrap.
What are the prizes?
🏆 1st Overall $750 cash + $1,500 in API credits
🏆 2nd Overall $500 cash + $1,000 in API credits
🏆 3rd Overall $250 cash + $500 in API credits
Honorable Mentions:
🌍 Most Impactful — $500 in API credits
🤖 Most Creative Use of Claude — $500 in API credits
When will prizes be distributed? After the event. Cash prize winners will be asked for their Org ID at the closing ceremony.
Mentorship & Support
Will there be mentors available? Yes — mentors will be available throughout hack time at a designated mentorship area in the venue. Feel free to walk over with questions about your project, technical blockers, or how to think about your problem space.
Who are the mentors? Mentors include experienced builders and Anthropic Campus Ambassadors who can help guide your project in the right direction. A full mentor list will be shared closer to the event.
What can I ask mentors for help with? Anything — scoping your idea, technical implementation, Claude API usage, ethical considerations, or just gut-checking your approach. They're there to help you build better.
Logistics
Will there be food? Yes — a full lunch buffet (pizza, iced tea, lemonade) at 12PM and snacks (muffi ns, croissants, coffee) at 3PM, fully catered by ASUCLA.
Will there be power strips? Yes — power strips will be available throughout the venue.
Will there be WiFi? Yes — reliable WiFi is available in the Grand Ackerman Ballroom. The password will be in your printed hacker guide on the day.
Will there be a printed hacker guide? Yes — you'll receive a printed guide with the schedule, prompt + tracks, rubric summary, and WiFi password at check-in.
Is there parking? Parking is available on campus — plan accordingly or take public transit if possible!!
